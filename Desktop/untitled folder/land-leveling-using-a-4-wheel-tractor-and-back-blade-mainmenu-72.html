Land leveling using a 4-wheel tractor and back blade

To level a field using a 4-wheel tractor and a back blade you need the following additional equipment:

  • plough (disc, moldboard or tine)
  • harrows (disc or tine)
  • tractor blade or drag bucket
  • surveying equipment

1. Plough the field.

Plough the field preferably from the center of the field outwards. It is preferable to plough the field when the soil is moist because if the soil is ploughed dry a significant increase in tractor power is required.  While it is best to plough when the soil is moist, disc or moldboard ploughs may be needed in hard dry conditions, Disc harrows or tine implements are ideal for second workings.

2. Mark the high and low spots in the field

  • A topographic survey will need to be undertaken to establish the high and low areas in the field.

 

  • A field diagram should be drawn noting the level of cut and fill required in the field.
  • From the field diagram, a strategy can be determined to most effectively move soil. It is difficult to move soil more than 50m with a back-blade. The field should be then marked with different colored markers noting areas of cut and areas of fill.  
  • If  leveling with water in the field, the water can be used to establish the high and low places in the field
 

Marking the high and low spots

 

3. Begin leveling

  • With the blade or drag bucket start at the highest point and drag soil to the lowest areas. It is best to add small wings to the end of the blade to improve the carriage of soil. Weight may also need to be added to the blade to improve the cutting ability. When returning to the high places lift the cutting blade above the ground level. Depth control wheels on the blade/bucket makes it easier to leave a more uniform finish. In wet areas where there is poor traction or a chance of bogging the tractor it may be better to push the soil rather than drag the soil.

Wet leveling with puddler

           

After the first leveling, survey the field and if necessary continue moving soil from the high to low areas.

Time

It will take at least two days to level one hectare in the first year using a blade and one day per hectare using a drag bucket.
